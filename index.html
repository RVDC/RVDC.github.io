Camp description:

This camp offers a full-day outdoor experience in a secure, supervised setting for girls age 6 and up. This year's theme centers on the "Space" experience and will include STEM activities, outdoor skills, and crafts and archery. We are planning a theatrical visit from a "space related historical figure" and some star gazing as well.

Information about how campers are placed into units/groups:

All units are age-based and will participate in camp-planned activities and events as well as enjoy free time to socialize and build friendships. All units are adult volunteer-led with young adult assistants Campers may request one buddy from their age level. Attempts will be made to honor the request only if both campers request one another, and all registration forms and fees are received in one envelope and ages are same level. We cannot accommodate requests that do not follow this procedure.

Program Aide Information:

Program Aide-In-Training (PAIT): Girls entering grade 7 in the fall who wish to work toward their PA pin. Girls will receive instruction as well as opportunities for hands-on leadership experience. They will attend an additional day of training at camp with PAs and adult volunteers. This is not a LiA Program.

Program Aide (PA): Girls entering grade 8 or higher who have completed council approved Program Aide training. This can serve as service hour units for the PA pin. Please complete the Day Camp Program Aide Application and one Program Aide Reference and submit with registration paperwork.

Volunteer In-Service: All adult volunteers, Program Aides, and Program Aides-in-training are required to attend our camp-wide in-service the Saturday before camp (Aug 4, 2108).

Volunteers: All adult volunteers must be registered, have a current background check, and complete trainings.

Boys and Tag-a-longs can attend camp if they are attending with a fulltime volunteer. Tag-a-longs are girls and boys ages 2-5, and must be potty-trained. Boys are defined as age 6-12.

Full week adult volunteers will receive a camp fee credit of $20 per child if the adult is registered and background checked by April 30. Those registered after April 30th will receive a refund of $10 per registered child after camp. For more information please contact the day camp director or day camp registrar.

Training will be Saturday August 4th and is required

Rainbow Valley/Sunset West Day Camp is 100% volunteer operated. To make camp successful, volunteers are asked to attend the entire week. Children of volunteers will be given placement priority. We offer a unit for volunteer's tagalongs All volunteers must be registered Girl Scouts, complete a background check, and complete required trainings.

Transportation: All campers must ride the bus, except children of volunteers. Finalized information regarding buses will be sent out with camper confirmation letters. More bus stops may be added as needed.
